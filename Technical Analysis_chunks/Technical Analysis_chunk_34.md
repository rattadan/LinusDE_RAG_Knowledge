**Original Text:**

There are several types of moving averages, each with distinct characteristics:

Simple Moving Average (SMA)
The SMA calculates the average price over a specific period by adding closing prices and dividing by the number of periods. While simple to understand, SMAs give equal weight to all prices in the calculation period. Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
EMAs, which are used in moving average convergence divergence calculations, place greater emphasis on recent prices. This makes EMAs more responsive to new information, allowing traders to react more quickly to market changes. Weighted Moving Average (WMA)
WMAs assign different weights to price data, with greater weight given to more recent prices. This creates a moving average that reacts faster to price changes than an SMA but is less volatile than an EMA.
